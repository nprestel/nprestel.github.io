title: Most of my life was spent trying to figure out how to do a $50 project for $0.50
---

Today I learned about Dave Gingery, a machinest and inventor, best known for his series of books on how to build machine tools. But if you look at his quotes and essays Mr. Gingery is a wealth of common sense knowledge, most of which can be applied to entrepreneurship and building stuff. One of his most famous quotes is 
> "I spent half my life trying to figure out how to do a fifty dollar job for fifty cents. I spent the other half looking for the fifty cents" 
>

What he means by this is when confronted in our lives by obstacles, sometimes in the form of lack of resources (in this example money), we spend much too long focused on them and give them too much credence. More often than not, these "apparent" obstacles are myths and either never existed or never impeded our progress. 

As Dave says...
>There is often too much discussion and too little practical work done. What is really needed is to put the whole matter into perspective so that apparent obstacles can be put aside and we can get on with the business at hand.

Solid advice indeed! Anyone who has ever tried to build anything substantial can relate.

I suggest anyone reading this click over to Dave Gingery's full essay on this topic [(link here)](http://gingerybooks.com/DaveGingeryEssay.html). It's a short but enlightening read.

I now plan on picking up his book series on machining!
