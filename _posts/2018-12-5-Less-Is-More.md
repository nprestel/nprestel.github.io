title: Less is more
---

The other day Wife and I made a pact to each other - we're going to buy fewer, but better, things. This came about after attempting to declutter our house and noticing how many things we have but no longer use. In other words, we decided to become smarter shoppers. Example: buying two Amazon Fire tablets (one for each of our kids) was a terrible decision in hindsight. If I could do it again I'd buy one iPad for both of them to share. The Amazon tablets are useless at this point. Two years later they're too slow, have no available memory left after all the updates, and transferring games from the cloud to each kid's profile requires a PhD. On the other hand, iPads just work. 

I'd like to extend this strategy into life and how we manage our free time. I've realized the past few months that having more of a good thing isn't always better. I've been working a 9-5 job that gets me home everyday and allows me to spend lots of time with the Wife and Kids. After a few years of this I think it's actually been a bad thing for me. Let's pause here, this is a hard thing for someone to say....that having too much time with their family has been bad for them. But let me expound on this. The more time I have to spend at home, the more I've found myself getting undisciplined and lazy, I've managed to get on everyone's nerves, and worst of all I've felt depression creep into my life.

I've come to the conlusion that someone with my personality and makeup does better with less, but more quality, home time. What gets in the way of this consensus is the stigma that comes with saying "I need to spend less time with my family to be a better partner/wife/husband/dad."
