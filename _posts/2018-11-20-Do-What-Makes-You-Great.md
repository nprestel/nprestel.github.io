As I get closer to 40 I've been catching myself in a state of retrospection over the good and bad things (i.e. _experiences_) that put me where I'm at today.

Two things stand out to me...

>__Number one__:  
There is an entire bucket-full of "goals" and "important things-to-do" that have piled up over the years

I've finally realized these goals aren't as important as I'd like to think (_or once believed_). Otherwise they'd be done, or at least worked on, by now. 
    _(I have to face it, no matter how much I'd like it to happen I won't be speaking fluent Mandarin by the end of 2019.)_ 

For too long I've been like the guy signing up for a new gym membership, fooling myself by being over-optimistic. Not over-optimistic about what __I can accomplish__. Rather, over-optimistic about what __I want to accomplish__. As hard as it is to say....my list of goals just isn't that important or exciting to me. Worse than that, it's become a 10,000 lb. weight I have to carry around.

But that's okay! Part of growing up is learning more about yourself and adapting. A side effect of this untouched list of "to-dos" is the feeling of disappointment that creeps up when things go unfinished. I've been fighting these feelings a lot over the years. What I've recently learned is to let go of all this extra baggage and just be happy with what I am today. This doesn't mean stop trying to learn and be better, it means stop chasing things I pretend to care about and focus on the fun and interesting stuff. Which leads me to my second observation...

>__Number two__: Do the stuff that makes you ~~happy~~ great

I'm careful here to use the word GREAT and not HAPPY. Because if everyone just did what makes them happy we'd have a lot more starving artists and a lot fewer plumbers and electricians. But doing what makes you GREAT means understanding what you're naturally good at __and__ also knowing this thing isn't something that's soul-sucking and will make you miserable. If you can find this overlap then you have something you should double down on and really nurture as a career. 

For me this intersection is Supply Chain Logistics. I kinda fell backwards into this field. I always thought it was just going to be a temporary stop as I transitioned into a full-fledged programmer. But fifteen years later I can say it's not so bad. And on top of that, I'm not so bad at it either. 

So after 40 years I've decided to cut the dead weight that's been holding me back and focus on the fun stuff. As someone smarter than me once said.. 
>It's better to go an inch wide and a mile deep, rather than a mile wide and an inch deep. 

Become a master - an expert in your industry.
