For my first post of 2019 I want to submit a list of everyday items that give back 10x more than they cost. If someone was to ask "what are some things you can't live without?" this would probably be my list. I'll come back and add/edit as I see fit in the future. Also, these are listed in no special order.

__Running shoes__
<br />I know I said this list isn't in any special order but running shoes are definitely my #1A. Strap on a pair of running shoes and you've got a ticket to adventure. I don't visit any new city without packing a pair of trail runners.  

__Power drill__
<br />If you're a new home owner, you better have one of these. Before you go buy a saw, a hammer, a wratchet set, a stud finder, a measuring tape, go get yourself a decent drill. You'll use this for everything...from hanging pictures to installing new door locks, and everything in between.

__Notebook & pen__
<br />Screw all note taking apps, they all suck. Even the best ones are still a layer of disruption between the thoughts in your head and getting them recorded. Nothing is as seamless as pen and paper. Want to take it to the next level? I've even developed a new brainstorming process. I've settled on a [magnetic notebook](https://www.rekonect.com/) with removable/replaceable pages that I can easily feed through a [portable scanner](https://www.amazon.com/Epson-WorkForce-DS-40-Wireless-Sheet-fed/dp/B00K33YZP0) - that can convert written word to type. So great.

__Kettle bell__
<br />My dad bought me oneas a birthday present about 7 years ago and I still use it everyday. It's such a simple piece of equipment but has so many uses. I actually never do the kettle bell swings with it, instead I do bicep/tricep curls, overhead presses, sumo squats, ab work, etc. I attribute the kettle bell for helping cure 10+ yrs of lower back issues that used to leave me paralyzed.

__A good pot__
<br />This is my "power drill" of the kitchen. If you pickup one decent quality pot, it'll save you a ton of money and kitchen counter space. Right now I'm rocking the [La Creuset 5.3L DO](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0076NOGPY/ref=cm_sw_em_r_mt_dp_U_fMFpCbGGHMB3T). It's quite expensive but also one of those items you buy once and pass down to your kids. But if you're looking for something more affordable, get yourself a nice big, heavy Lodge cast iron [pot](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B000LEXR0K/ref=cm_sw_em_r_mt_dp_U_rOFpCbY27V50Q) or [pan](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B07C54BX2M/ref=cm_sw_em_r_mt_dp_U_CPFpCbTH340XA). These things are a swiss army knife for the kitchen. You can bake cakes, deep fry chicken, make soups and stews, perfectly cook chicken, sear steaks, and much more. 

__Travel detergent__
<br />I'm no longer worried if I need to travel for more than a few days. With a bottle of [this small, concentrated soap](https://www.rei.com/product/407166/campsuds-campsuds-2-oz) in tow I can clean my workout and underclothes dozens of times. I've become an expert at hotel sink washing.

__Buff__
<br />Get yourself one of [these](https://www.rei.com/b/buff). In winter it's perfect for covering your neck and face (like a balaclava), or for using as ear protection. In the summer these are great for keeping sweat off your face, for wearing under a bike helmet, or for dipping in cold water and putting around your neck on those long runs.

__Blender__
<br />For someone who hates meal planning a blender is my best friend. Having one of these around means you're never leaving the house without breakfast. Get a cheap blender and keep a supply of bananas, peanut butter, almond milk, nutella, blueberries, oranges, and some frozen mangos on hand. You'll be good to go.

__Computer terminal__
<br />For the non-nerds out there who want to get into programming, I bet a lot of you aren't aware your Macs come fully furnished with Python and Java languages ready to go. Programming languages are like the pen and paper equivalent for the digital artist. Come up with a good app idea and learn how to code. That's what I did and I've never regretted it.
