Ground

Ground (shipping mode)

What is it?

Also called Full Truckload. Truckload typically refers to moving material using a semi-truck over the road. This method differs from Less Than Truckload Mode in that the shipper has paid to use the entire trailer and is not sharing space with anyone else.

Advantages

  1. Speed - fairly fast compared to other shipping options
  2. Reliability - trucks rarely break down and when they do it’s not for very long
  3. Low Damage - damage is relatively low compared to other shipping options
  4. Accessibility - trucks can go anywhere there’s a road
  5. Service - offers Door to Door loading and unloading

Disadvantages

  1. Coverage - limited to continental transport
  2. Weather - sensitive to the elements
  3. Restrictions - must abide by cargo limits and hours of service guidelines

Equipment Types

Semi-trucks are made up of two pieces: the tractor (i.e. power unit) and one or more trailers. Listed below are the most common trailer sizes in North America.

  1. 53’ Trailer (most popular for Full Truckload)
  2. 48’ Trailer
  3. 45’ Trailer
  4. 33’ Trailer
  5. 28’ Trailer (most popular for Less Than Truckload)
  6. Other types: Flatbed Trailer, Tank Trailer, Refridgerated Trailer (a.k.a. Reefer), Intermodal Trailer
